### Semantics of Empire: Ottoman Turkish Translator
*2024 · Retired*

A neural machine translation model for Ottoman Turkish, built as part of my Symbolic Systems M.S. project at Stanford. The translator was trained to handle 18th–19th century Ottoman administrative and literary texts, a domain largely absent from existing NLP resources.

The project ran as a free public tool and was used by scholars and students across the Ottoman and Middle Eastern Studies fields before being retired due to API costs.

[Read the blog post](/2024/05/01/GPT4-translator/) · [View archived repo](https://github.com/mervetekgurler)

### Cistern: A Database of Geographical Knowledge in the Ottoman World
*2020–present · Active*

A collaborative database of geographical knowledge produced in and about the Ottoman world, co-created with Adrien Zakar. Cistern aggregates placename data from historical sources to support spatial humanities research.

[geocistern.github.io](https://geocistern.github.io/)
